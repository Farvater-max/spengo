Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

Overview

SpenGo is a personal expense tracker that stores all your data directly in your own Google Spreadsheet. We do not operate any servers, databases, or backend infrastructure that handles your personal or financial information.

What we access

When you sign in with Google, SpenGo requests permission to:

Create a Google Spreadsheet Read & edit that spreadsheet Search Drive for that file

We request only the minimum scopes necessary: spreadsheets and drive.file. The drive.file scope grants access only to files created by SpenGo — not your entire Drive.

What we never do

Sell your data Share with third parties Use for advertising Access other Drive files Store data on our servers

Your data stays yours

All expense records are written exclusively to a Google Spreadsheet in your personal Google Drive account. You own this file and can view, edit, export, or delete it at any time directly in Google Sheets.

If you stop using SpenGo, your data remains in your Drive untouched. Revoking SpenGo's access in your Google Account settings immediately removes our ability to read or modify the file.

Authentication

Sign-in is handled entirely by Google OAuth 2.0. SpenGo never sees, requests, or stores your Google password. Authentication tokens are managed by the browser session and the Google Identity Services library.

Local storage

SpenGo stores your selected language preference and a temporary cache of recent expenses in your browser's localStorage. This data never leaves your device and can be cleared at any time by clearing your browser's site data.

Google API policy

SpenGo's use of Google APIs complies with the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We use data obtained via Google APIs solely to provide the expense tracking functionality described in this policy.

Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, feel free to reach out via the project repository or open an issue on GitHub.
